Drycleaning Process

Dry cleaners rely on a different non-water-based solvent to remove stains. First of all, your clothes are added to a front-loading washer. The fabric is tumbled, relying on friction to scrub out the difficult stains with the solvent. After the fabric is out of the front-loading washer, an extractor removes the solvent from the clothes and machine, along with the dirt and grime, leaving your clothes perfectly clean. To make certain that your clothes are clean, small amounts of “wet” or “dry” spot removing products are applied by hand for the toughest spots.

We Press!

Three pressing machines are shown in this picture. With every wash, there is a press! A pressing machine is a machine for pressing a garment or section between two contoured and heated pressure surfaces that may have steam and vacuum systems in either or both surfaces.
